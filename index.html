Outlet Monitor  ·  Mossy River Dam

Water exits through the Mossy River dam at Terin's Landing. When the lake is high, the river channel itself — not the dam — limits outflow to ~500 cfs. In 2011, inflow peaked at 60,000 cfs. The lake rose.

Basin Snowpack — Contributing Context

The basin entered melt season with 160–185 mm SWE — the highest recorded snowpack near Riding Mountain. That water is now actively moving through the watershed. The high-elevation headwaters (Wilson River, Mink Creek) broke on April 14. Residual snowpack continues to contribute as melt progresses.
